The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) has announced that H-1B cap-subject registration for fiscal year 2027 will open **March 4 through March 18, 2026**.

## What's Different in 2026

This year's H-1B lottery introduces a significant departure from prior years: **wage-based selection rules** are now in effect. Rather than purely random selection among all registrations, USCIS will prioritize registrations based on the offered wage level relative to the prevailing wage for the occupation and location.

### The Four Wage Tiers

USCIS divides wage offers into four levels:

- **Level IV** (highest): Workers paid at or above the 95th percentile of the prevailing wage
- **Level III**: Workers paid between the 67th and 95th percentile
- **Level II**: Workers paid between the 33rd and 67th percentile
- **Level I** (lowest): Workers paid below the 33rd percentile

USCIS will select from higher wage tiers first until the cap is met. This means workers offered higher salaries — or workers in high-wage occupations — have better odds of selection.

## What Employers Need to Do Now

If you're an employer planning to sponsor H-1B workers this cycle, here's your action checklist:

1. **Register your attorneys now** — Ensure your immigration counsel has access to the USCIS H-1B registrant system before March 4
2. **Confirm wage compliance** — Work with your HR and payroll teams to ensure offered wages meet or exceed the prevailing wage for the role and location
3. **Prepare supporting documentation** — Specialty occupation letters, degree evaluations, and job descriptions should be ready before registration
4. **Budget for premium processing** — If selection is made, premium processing ($2,805) ensures a decision within 15 business days

## What Workers Need to Know

If you're an H-1B hopeful this year:

- Your **employer sponsors you** — you cannot self-petition for an H-1B
- If your employer's registration is selected, they have until **June 30, 2026** to file the full petition
- If approved, your H-1B status begins **October 1, 2026**
- If not selected, explore **cap-exempt alternatives** — O-1, TN (for Canadians and Mexicans), or E-3 (for Australians) may apply to your situation

## Alternatives If You Don't Get Selected

The H-1B lottery is not the only path. Watson Immigration Law regularly helps clients evaluate:

- **O-1 visa** — For individuals with extraordinary ability in their field
- **L-1 visa** — For employees being transferred from a foreign affiliate
- **E-2 visa** — For treaty country nationals investing in a U.S. business
- **EB-5 visa** — For investors seeking permanent residence
