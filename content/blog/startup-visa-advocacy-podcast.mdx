In this episode, I sit down with a prominent voice in the startup visa movement to discuss where immigration reform stands today, what the prospects are for a dedicated startup visa, and what founders can do right now to navigate the current system.

## Topics covered

- The current state of H-1B reform discussions
- What a startup visa bill would actually look like
- Why EB-2 NIW has become increasingly popular with startup founders
- The SelectUSA program and how it helps international entrepreneurs
- How founders from countries without E-2 treaties (India, China, Brazil) can still build in the U.S.

## Key takeaways

The startup visa conversation is gaining momentum. Multiple startup visa bills have been introduced in Congress over the past decade, and with each election cycle, the prospects shift. What hasn't shifted is the reality: international founders are launching billion-dollar companies in other countries because the U.S. immigration system makes it too hard for them to stay here.

Until a dedicated startup visa exists, the best options remain O-1A, EB-2 NIW, and E-2 — all of which we discuss in detail in this episode.
