The results are in. If you weren't selected in the H-1B lottery, you are not alone — and you are not out of options.

The H-1B lottery remains one of the most frustrating parts of U.S. immigration. In recent years, USCIS has received 400,000+ registrations for just 85,000 visa slots. The odds are brutal. But the good news: there are real, substantive alternatives.

## What Are Your Options?

### O-1A Visa — Extraordinary Ability

If you have achieved national or international recognition in your field, the O-1A may be your best alternative. There is no annual cap, no lottery, and it can be filed at any time of year.

You may qualify if you have:
- Been cited significantly by peers in your field
- Published research or articles in recognized publications
- Received industry awards or recognition
- Commanded a high salary relative to peers
- Been invited to judge others' work (peer review, competitions, hackathons)
- Been featured in media coverage about your work
- Held a critical role at a distinguished organization

### Cap-Exempt H-1B

Certain employers are not subject to the H-1B cap and can file year-round, including universities, nonprofit research organizations, and government research entities. If you can secure employment at one of these organizations, you can get an H-1B without the lottery.

### L-1 Visa — Intracompany Transfer

If you have worked for a foreign parent, subsidiary, or affiliate of a U.S. company for at least 1 year in the past 3 years, an L-1 transfer may be possible. L-1A (for managers/executives) is particularly powerful because it leads directly to an EB-1C green card without PERM labor certification.

### TN Visa (Canadians and Mexicans)

Canadian and Mexican nationals in qualifying occupations can obtain TN status under USMCA (formerly NAFTA) without a lottery, without employer petition processing, and without an annual cap. TN is available at the border or at U.S. consulates for certain professional roles including engineers, accountants, scientists, and more.

### EB-2 National Interest Waiver

If your work has national importance and you have an advanced degree or exceptional ability, the EB-2 National Interest Waiver lets you self-petition for a green card without an employer and without a PERM labor certification. This is particularly powerful for startup founders, researchers, and independent professionals.

## Should I Try Again Next Year?

If the O-1A, L-1, TN, and NIW don't fit your situation, then yes — the H-1B is still worth trying. But I would encourage you not to sit idle waiting for next March. Use this time to build your O-1A evidence, explore whether your employer has a cap-exempt affiliate, or investigate whether an EB-2 NIW case is feasible.
